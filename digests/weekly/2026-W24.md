The micro-market that diverged most from its parent in Week 24 is **UTC**, whose Q1 correction far outpaced San Diego city's aggregate attached performance.

## Sentiment Trendline

No area shifted sentiment during the inaugural tracking window (June 12–15). Established baselines:

- **Seller-favored:** Carmel Valley, Loudoun County, Fairfax County, Oakton HS area
- **Balanced:** San Diego city, Arcola (20166)
- **Buyer-favored:** UTC, Dallas, Austin

**Builder signals:** NoVA builder incentives are at a reported 5-year high (price cuts up to 5%, rate buydowns, $10K closing cost credits), signaling builders anticipate softening demand — a leading indicator for resale price pressure in the next 3–6 months. Arcola (20166) new construction is sold out, so this lever is absent there; resale pricing is supported by supply scarcity. Austin and Dallas builders are offering $10K–$30K incentive packages via rate buydowns and design credits — historically, builder incentives in these markets lead resale price cuts by one to two quarters.

## Implications & Positioning

**For a buyer:** UTC is the highest near-term opportunity in California — condos down 11.9% YoY, 38 DOM, time to negotiate. Arcola (20166) offers the best rent-vs.-own math in scope ($389/mo gap, 6.8% gross yield), though the resale pool is thin. The Oakton HS area candidates need HOA and FCPS boundary verification before any offer. Dallas and Austin offer the most leverage, with Austin's 52% price-cut rate and Dallas's 7.7-month condo supply giving buyers genuine negotiating power.

**For a seller/investor:** San Diego city and Loudoun County sellers retain pricing power (sell-to-list ~99–100%). Fairfax County's 15-week inventory build is a warning for condo sellers; the -2.7% YoY condo forecast adds to the pressure. Texas sellers face meaningful headwinds (DOM 48–75, deep price-cut share). For investors, Arcola's 6.8% gross yield with the tightest rent-to-own gap is the most compelling cash-flow candidate in scope.
